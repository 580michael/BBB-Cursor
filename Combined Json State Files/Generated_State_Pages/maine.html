What happens if someone misses a court date after being bailed out in Maine?
Missing a court date after posting bail in Maine has serious consequences. The court will likely issue a bench warrant for immediate arrest, and the full bail amount becomes due from the bail bond company. The bondsman will then seek reimbursement from you and any co-signers, potentially using collection agencies or legal action. Additionally, any collateral used to secure the bond may be forfeited. In Maine, defendants who miss court dates often face additional charges of failure to appear, which can result in higher bail amounts or even bail denial for future arrests.
